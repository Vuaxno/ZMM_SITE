Career Description

AI Engineer

AI Engineers build and deploy intelligent systems that solve real problems at scale. The work sits at the intersection of software engineering, data science, and machine learning, taking models from research into production environments where they actually run.

The field is expanding fast, driven by advances in large language models, computer vision, and automation. Organizations across every industry are building AI infrastructure, and the demand for engineers who can design, deploy, and maintain those systems is growing with it.

My path into this field starts with a technical foundation at UNO and real enterprise experience at Omnicom, where I work inside the kind of systems that AI is already beginning to transform.

Personal Statement

Academic and Professional Journey

I have always been drawn to systems. The way things connect, operate under pressure, and fail in ways nobody expected. Growing up in Omaha, I watched technology reshape the industries around me while a path into that world felt out of reach. That distance became the reason I started. I chose Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence because it was the hardest, most meaningful commitment I could make to the future I wanted.

What I value most is growth that actually shows up in the work. My internship at Omnicom put me inside enterprise IT operations as a sophomore. I was resolving real system failures, managing access control for a global organization, and automating processes with PowerShell. That experience taught me more about how intelligent systems break, and how to keep them from breaking, than a classroom setting alone could offer.

At AutoZone I managed a team and learned that leadership is mostly about execution and consistency. At A&B Team Underground I handled client communication and project coordination in a physically demanding field. Both taught me that professionalism travels with you regardless of the setting.

My strongest skill is working across the technical and the human. I can build something in code and explain it clearly to someone who has never touched a terminal. I think in systems, plan in timelines, and measure my progress against the highest standard I can find. AI Engineering is where those things converge, and it is what I am working toward.
